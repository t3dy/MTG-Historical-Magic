Word Histories

Long-form pages on where each magical word came from — its origin, etymology, and historical usage — grounded in the medieval- and renaissance-magic databases and the PDF library, and cited.

How these are researched

Each history is built with scripts/research.py, which searches Ted's magic/alchemy databases (Renaissance definitions + concordance, MedievalMagicDB, TheosophicalAlchemyDB) and the full-text PDF corpora in E:\pdf (medieval magic, renaissance magic, alchemy) for the term, returning a citable dossier. Every term's Etymology tab also auto-surfaces what the scholarship says. Method: docs/RESEARCH_METHOD.md.

For terms whose modern study is itself contested — shaman, alchemy, fetish, and 48 others — a ⚑ Historiographical note on the term's Origin & History tab engages the live scholarly debate rather than a settled story. Every work cited across the site, ranked by how often it's used, is on the Sources page.
