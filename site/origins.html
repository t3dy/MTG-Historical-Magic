The Loanword Atlas

English did not invent its magic — it borrowed it. Every term in the study, sorted by the language it ultimately came from.

Why it matters

Each borrowing is a history. Greek gives us the learned, priestly words — necromancer, hierophant, the whole -mancy family of divinations. Latin supplies the ritual verbs of the Church and the law — conjure, consecrate, invoke. Arabic carries the science — alchemy, elixir, azoth — each still wearing its al-. And the oldest, homeliest magic is Old English: witch, warlock, spell, besom. The map of where magic-words come from is a map of who Europe thought its magicians were.
